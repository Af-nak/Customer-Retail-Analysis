## Project Overview
This project analyzes retail sales data across multiple shopping malls to uncover customer behavior, sales trends, and revenue drivers. The goal was to transform raw transactional data into meaningful insights that support strategic planning, customer targeting, and sales optimization.


## Business Problem
The primary business challenge was to understand shopping behavior and sales performance at a glance in order to support better decision-making. Specifically, the analysis aimed to::
- Sales performance over time and across shopping malls  
- Key customer segments contributing to revenue  
- Product categories driving the highest sales  
- The influence of payment methods and demographics on purchasing behavior  

A key challenge in the dataset was uneven time coverage, as data for 2023 only spans January to March, while 2021 and 2022 contain full-year records.


## Tools
- **Power BI** – Data cleaning, modeling, visualization, and reporting


## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
The exploratory phase focused on answering key business questions that guided deeper analysis:

- How does sales performance change over time?
- Which months show peak and low performance?
- What is the gender and age distribution of customers?
- Which payment methods are most commonly used?
- Which product categories and malls generate the highest revenue?

This stage helped identify patterns, trends, and dataset limitations.


## Data Analysis
The following visualizations and metrics were used:

- **KPIs**  
  Key metrics such as total sales, total revenue, quantity sold, and customer count were displayed for quick performance tracking.

- **Line Chart**  
  Used to analyze sales trends over time. The apparent sales decline is influenced by incomplete 2023 data.  
  For full-year periods (2021–2022), February shows the lowest sales, while July records the highest performance.

- **Doughnut Chart**  
  Used to analyze gender distribution and payment methods. Female customers account for 59.7% of total shoppers, and cash is the most frequently used payment method.

- **Bar Chart**  
  Used to evaluate product category and mall performance. Clothing consistently performs best and generates the highest revenue across malls.

- **Water Fall**  
  Used to show how Top Category Impact Overall Performance
  

## Key Insights
- The perceived sales drop over time is primarily due to incomplete 2023 data rather than actual underperformance.
- February consistently records the lowest sales, while July shows peak performance in complete-year data.
- Female shoppers dominate purchasing activity, contributing 59.7% of total sales.
- Cash is the most preferred payment method.
- Millennials (under 44) are the most active shoppers, while Boomers represent the smallest segment.
- Clothing is the highest revenue-generating product category across all malls.
- Istanbul is the top-performing mall in terms of overall sales and revenue.


## Business Value
This analysis helps stakeholders:
- Interpret sales trends accurately by accounting for data gaps
- Identify high-value customer segments for targeted marketing
- Focus on top-performing products and locations
- Improve seasonal planning and sales strategies


## Recommendations
- Launch marketing campaigns earlier in the year, ahead of peak seasons, to engage customers before demand peaks.
- Prioritize inventory and promotions around Clothing, as it is the strongest revenue driver.
- Focus customer engagement strategies on female and millennial shoppers.
- Maintain or enhance cash-based payment incentives, given its popularity.
- Use complete-year datasets for year-over-year comparisons to avoid misleading conclusions.
